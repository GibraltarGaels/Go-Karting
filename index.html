Pricewise: Will Peters' Stag Go-Karting Classic

By Tom Segal (sort of)

The Will Peters Stag Go-Karting Grand Prix promises thrills, spills, and post-race beers. With five drivers confirmed and one wildcard looming, the betting markets are buzzing. Here's the lowdown on the runners and where the value lies.

Joel Orme (4/1)

Smooth and consistent, Joel is the favourite for good reason. But at a short price, is he really value in a field of unknowns?

Verdict: Likely to lead early. Vulnerable late.

Emmett Leavy (4/6)

Blistering pace, unpredictable temperament. Could blitz the field... or bin it at the first bend.

Verdict: High risk, high reward.

Shane Callaghan (3/1)

Calculated and cunning, Shane plays the long game. Not flashy, but fiercely effective.

Verdict: Solid each-way value. Definitely in the mix.

Will Peters (The Stag) (5/1)

More likely to be holding a beer than a trophy. Fun to watch, but not to back.

Verdict: Not today, Your Majesty.

Sam Thompson (2/1)

Bringing his own gloves? That’s commitment. But will it translate to results?

Verdict: Danger to all, but possibly flammable under pressure.

Michael Stephen Charge (TBC) (20/1 if declared)

Yet to be confirmed, but a potential dark horse. Don't ignore the Charge.

Verdict: Market mover if entered.

Pricewise Verdict

Recommended Bet: Shane Callaghan – 1pt win @ 3/1
Wildcard Bet: Michael Stephen Charge – 0.5pt EW @ 20/1 (if declared)
